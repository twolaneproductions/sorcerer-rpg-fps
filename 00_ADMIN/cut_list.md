# cut_list.md
## Ashenmoor — Cut List
**Gate Shaker Press**
**Version:** 0.1
**Last Updated:** 2026-04-26
**Status:** Living document — updated throughout production

---

## Purpose

This document holds everything that has been removed from active scope. Nothing here is permanently rejected — items are moved here when they risk the schedule or the one-hour target, not because they are bad ideas. Review this list at the end of each phase. Some items will be reinstated. Most will become post-launch candidates.

**The rule:** If you are spending time on something not in active scope, stop and add it here instead.

---

## Cut from Active Scope

### Levels / Zones

**Blighted Moor and Sunken Abbey**
*Reason:* Optional zone adds 10-15 minutes to a one-hour game, requires unique enemy behavior (lesser wraiths, Ghostlight Torch mechanic), and a full interior environment (the abbey). The narrative content housed here — the memorial tablets, the covered portrait mystery — can be redistributed to existing zones or held for a post-launch update.
*Post-launch potential:* High. The zone is fully designed and would be a natural first content update.

**Watchtower as full encounter**
*Reason:* Clearing a goblin camp to reach a viewpoint is a meaningful side activity in a longer game. In a one-hour game it competes with pacing. Reduced to an environmental landmark visible from the Greywood road.
*Post-launch potential:* Low — likely absorbed into a broader Greywood expansion if the moor is reinstated.

---

### Weapons

**The Twin Wyrm** (Super Shotgun equivalent)
*Reason:* Seven weapons is the right number for one hour. The Scatterstaff covers the close-range burst role. The Twin Wyrm is a power-fantasy duplicate of the same function and loses the argument.
*Post-launch potential:* Moderate — a good addition if a harder difficulty mode or new zone is added.

**The Siege Spire** (Ballista equivalent)
*Reason:* The precision single-shot role is already partially covered by the Hex Twig's infinite-ammo fallback. The Siege Spire's implementation complexity (Spire Bolt crafting system) is disproportionate to a one-hour game.
*Post-launch potential:* High — the crafting mechanic is interesting and worth revisiting.

**The Unraveling Eye** (Unmakyr equivalent)
*Reason:* Requires the Eternity Shard secret cache system to function meaningfully. Secret hunting in a one-hour game is not a priority. Without the secret system the weapon loses its identity.
*Post-launch potential:* High — reinstate alongside a proper secret system in a content update.

**The Anima Vessel** (Soul Cube equivalent)
*Reason:* The kill-charge mechanic is the most complex weapon implementation in the original design. The payoff — an autonomous seeking projectile — is satisfying but the development cost is high relative to a one-hour game's weapon exposure time.
*Post-launch potential:* High. The most mechanically interesting weapon in the cut list.

---

### Armor

**Hexweave Coat**
*Reason:* Magic damage reduction is a useful armor property in a game with enough magical enemies to make it felt. In a one-hour game, the player encounters magical enemies late — not enough playtime for this piece to register meaningfully.
*Post-launch potential:* Moderate.

**Cowl of the Unseen**
*Reason:* Stealth-adjacent mechanics require enemy AI tuning for detection range. Out of scope for initial release.
*Post-launch potential:* High if stealth is ever developed as a system.

**Gauntlet of Deflection**
*Reason:* A physical block mechanic requires its own input and feedback system. Implementation cost exceeds value in a one-hour game.
*Post-launch potential:* Moderate.

**Swiftstrider Boots**
*Reason:* Movement speed modification interacts with GZDoom's movement model in ways that require careful tuning. A nice addition once core systems are stable.
*Post-launch potential:* High — low effort reinstatement once the game ships.

**Talisman of the Ancestors**
*Reason:* Passive health regen is a meaningful mechanic in a long game. In a one-hour game the player rarely has time between encounters for it to register. Effect too subtle for the runtime.
*Post-launch potential:* Low — likely rolled into another item's effect if reinstated.

---

### NPCs and Content

**Covered portrait mystery (Thornwall Castle throne room)**
*Reason:* Requires backstory content housed in the cut Sunken Abbey to pay off. Without the abbey tablets, the covered portrait is an unanswered question with no answer available. Reduced to pure atmosphere — it remains covered, it remains unexplained, no NPC will discuss it.
*Post-launch potential:* High — the natural centerpiece of a lore expansion.

**Emmet the page's full conversation tree**
*Reason:* Three of Emmet's dialogue branches reference content from the blighted moor and the watchtower encounter. Trimmed to the core beats — the King's mood, Mira's habits, the sparrow story.
*Post-launch potential:* N/A — restored automatically if cut zones are reinstated.

---

### Systems

**Secret cache system**
*Reason:* Secrets require level design time allocated specifically to their placement and reward. Out of scope for initial release. Without secrets, the Unraveling Eye and Eternity Shard system cannot function as designed.
*Post-launch potential:* High — a natural second-pass activity once the base game ships.

**Voice acting**
*Reason:* Budget and timeline. Text-only dialogue for initial release.
*Post-launch potential:* High — a voiced update is the single most impactful post-launch addition available.

**Manual save**
*Reason:* Checkpoint autosave only. Manual saving requires additional UI and state management. The three checkpoint locations are sufficient for a one-hour game.
*Post-launch potential:* Low — checkpoint system is appropriate for the game's design intent.

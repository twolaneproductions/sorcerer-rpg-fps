You are a sorcerer, venturing into the belly of your enemy’s castle in a demonic realm, where they are torturing your estranged master, attempting to possess his soul. You climb up out of the moat, through the gate, past the various wards, into the dungeon, where your enemies await. You must do battle with them. After freeing your master, a demon knight severs your head, compelling your master to channel the last bits of his soul into you, granting you the power to destroy the knight and the pillars of the castle, burying you all beneath the rubble.

## Magic

* The Pistol → Hex Twig
  * A bare, unadorned apprentice's wand. Single-shot arcane bolts with no frills.
  * Low damage, infinite minor mana
  * your fallback when everything else runs dry. Never impressive, always reliable.
* The Shotgun → Scatterstaff
  * A gnarled, wide-mouthed staff that fires a cone of bone-white bone pellets.
  * Devastating at close range, diffuse at a distance.
  * Ammunition: Bone Pellets, commonly collected from dead enemies.
* The Super Shotgun → The Twin Wyrm
  * A forked, double-headed staff that discharges two simultaneous blasts of dragon-breath force.
  * Enormous close-range damage, punishing reload pause.
  * Ammunition: Dragonfire Essence, medium-rare.
* The Chaingun → The Tempest Rod
  * A spinning, gyroscopic wand that spools up before unleashing a sustained torrent of lightning micro-bolts.
  * Ammunition: Storm Flux, drains fast.
* The Rocket Launcher → The Comet Staff
  * Fires a slow, glowing sphere of compressed elemental force that detonates on impact in a satisfying burst of fire and shockwave.
  * Splash damage, self-damage risk, same as always.
  * Ammunition: Comet Cores — heavy, limited, found in strongboxes and boss drops.
* The Plasma Rifle → The Magestream Wand
  * Rapid-fire bolts of raw violet arcane plasma, slightly homing, filling the air with light.
  * Ammunition: Arcane Plasma Vials — moderately common, but the fire rate will eat through them.
* The BFG 9000 → The Obliterator Stave
  * A massive, two-handed obsidian staff with a pulsing green eye at its crown.
  * Charges for two full seconds before releasing a sphere of world-ending necrotic force that rolls through the room, spawning tendrils of death on everything it passes.
  * Ammunition: Soul Confluence — extraordinarily rare, single charges found as level rewards or secret caches.
* The Chainsaw → The Hungering Branch
  * A twisted, living-wood wand covered in thorns that, when pressed against an enemy, endlessly feeds on their life force.
  * Melee-range only.
  * Converts enemy vitality directly into mana for your other weapons.
  * Ammunition: None.
* The Ballista (Doom Eternal) → The Siege Spire
  * A long, angular staff that fires a single bolt of concentrated arcane force at extreme velocity — punching through multiple enemies in a line.
  * Slow fire rate, surgical precision.
  * Ammunition: Spire Bolts
* The Unmakyr (Doom Eternal) → The Unraveling Eye
  * A strange, artifact-like wand with an unsettling organic quality.
  * Fires a sustained beam of pure unmaking energy.
  * Ammunition: Eternity Shards — only found in the deepest, most dangerous secret areas.
* The Soul Cube (Doom 3) → The Anima Vessel
  * A hovering, orbiting relic rather than a held wand.
  * Charges by absorbing the death-energy of five consecutive kills, then unleashes itself as an autonomous seeking projectile that homes in on the largest nearby threat and annihilates it, returning health to the caster.

## Part One

* Start approaching the gates of the TOWN. Still out in the woods, we come across a couple in trouble with some demons. We intervene (combat tutorial) to save them and then escort them to the gates of town.
* Passing through town, we approach the Castle. The guards will not admit us, disbelieving that we are the sorcerer's apprentice. We zap one of them with a little magic and are granted access.
* Once inside the Castle, we are granted access to the royal family. The King informs us about the fate of our master. He tells us that he went North to confront the Banished Lord, and has not returned. The King further states that if we can return with proof that the Banished has been slain, we will be mightily rewarded.
* After equipping ourselves in town, we head out through the wilderness.
* In the woods, we encounter goblins and demonic wolves.
* Arriving at the castle ruins, we find it inaccessible.
* ...
* The player, **SORCERER**, appears, teleported, into the murky waters of a **moat**. He is disoriented and not sure in which direction the surface lies (it's not up) but must swim to it before he runs out of air.
* After emerging onto the embankment, Sorcerer must cross the **drawbridge** and battle past a couple of **sentries** in the barbican before passing through the gate to enter the courtyard.
  * Inside the castle, we encounter skeletons, zombies, and various monsters.
* Inside the **courtyard**, there are a handful of **soldiers** and **demonic peasants** still hanging around the fires, whom Sorcerer must kill.  
* Inside the **hall**, he must pass through a small feast, dispatching those present.  
* In the **chambers**, there are a **demonic prince and princess** who put up a fight against the sorcerer. After ridding the world of them, the Sorcerer must not overlook the **hatchway** leading down to the dungeons.  
* Inside the **dungeon**, the airs streak with the lights of deeper magical fires and the echoes of Latin can be heard growing as Sorcerer travels deeper and deeper. He must fight **a small horde of demonic knights** outside of the door to the deepest cell.  
* Breaking into the depths of the dungeon, Sorcerer faces **his master**, another human sorcerer, forming a **portal** before a demonic warlock, with the **Demonic Knight Lord** standing beside.  
  * As Sorcerer tries to interrupt the formation of the portal, the Lord beheads him, causing the player to fall lifelessly to the floor. But as the portal finally snaps open, the severed head on the floor begins muttering incantations, bringing the body back to life, though it first begins spewing out blood, then magical sparks, as Sorcerer’s body fires of spells **destroying the Lord, the Warlock, the Master, and finally the Portal and the foundations of the castle**, burying all the demonic corpses and himself.

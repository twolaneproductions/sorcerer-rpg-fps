# Gameplay

* Magic “ammunition” where each different “ether” or “elixir” refills the “ammunition” for different magic spells  
  * ethers  
  * magic water, sage's elixir, or elfin elixir  
    * phoenix elixir for fire spells  
    * elixir of Thor for electicity

# Weapons

## Physical Weapons

* Longbow -- longest range
* Flail -- shortest range
* Repeating Arbalest -- "machine gun" mechanics

## Magic

* The Pistol → Witch Switch
  * A bare, unadorned apprentice's wand. Single-shot arcane bolts with no frills.
  * Low damage, infinite minor mana
  * your fallback when everything else runs dry. Never impressive, always reliable.
* The Shotgun → Scatterstaff
  * A gnarled, wide-mouthed staff that fires a cone of bone-white bone pellets.
  * Devastating at close range, diffuse at a distance.
  * Ammunition: Bone Pellets, commonly collected from dead enemies.
* The Super Shotgun → The Twin Wyrm
  * A forked, double-headed staff that discharges two simultaneous blasts of dragon-breath force.
  * Enormous close-range damage, punishing reload pause.
  * Ammunition: Dragonfire Essence, medium-rare.
* The Chaingun → The Tempest Rod
  * A spinning, gyroscopic wand that spools up before unleashing a sustained torrent of lightning micro-bolts.
  * Ammunition: Storm Flux, drains fast.
* The Rocket Launcher → The Comet Staff
  * Fires a slow, glowing sphere of compressed elemental force that detonates on impact in a satisfying burst of fire and shockwave.
  * Splash damage, self-damage risk, same as always.
  * Ammunition: Comet Cores — heavy, limited, found in strongboxes and boss drops.
* The Plasma Rifle → The Magestream Wand
  * Rapid-fire bolts of raw violet arcane plasma, slightly homing, filling the air with light.
  * Ammunition: Arcane Plasma Vials — moderately common, but the fire rate will eat through them.
* The BFG 9000 → The Obliterator Stave
  * A massive, two-handed obsidian staff with a pulsing green eye at its crown.
  * Charges for two full seconds before releasing a sphere of world-ending necrotic force that rolls through the room, spawning tendrils of death on everything it passes.
  * Ammunition: Soul Confluence — extraordinarily rare, single charges found as level rewards or secret caches.
* The Chainsaw → The Hungering Branch
  * A twisted, living-wood wand covered in thorns that, when pressed against an enemy, endlessly feeds on their life force.
  * Melee-range only.
  * Converts enemy vitality directly into mana for your other weapons.
  * Ammunition: None.
* The Ballista (Doom Eternal) → The Siege Spire
  * A long, angular staff that fires a single bolt of concentrated arcane force at extreme velocity — punching through multiple enemies in a line.
  * Slow fire rate, surgical precision.
  * Ammunition: Spire Bolts
* The Unmakyr (Doom Eternal) → The Unraveling Eye
  * A strange, artifact-like wand with an unsettling organic quality.
  * Fires a sustained beam of pure unmaking energy.
  * Ammunition: Eternity Shards — only found in the deepest, most dangerous secret areas.
* The Soul Cube (Doom 3) → The Anima Vessel
  * A hovering, orbiting relic rather than a held wand.
  * Charges by absorbing the death-energy of five consecutive kills, then unleashes itself as an autonomous seeking projectile that homes in on the largest nearby threat and annihilates it, returning health to the caster.

* Wand
    * Rapid-fire Ice slivers
      * slow and eventually freeze targets solid.
      * Ammunition: Glacial Mana Crystals.
    * Chain Lighting
      * sustained-fire
      * chains between nearby enemies

# Armor

## Worn / Body

* Apprentice's Robe — Your starting armor. Thin, unenchanted wool.
* Warded Travel Cloak — A heavy cloak with subtle runic stitching along the hem.
  * Reduces physical damage.
  * Rarely deflects arrows/swords.
  * Found or purchased in town.
* Hexweave Coat — A mid-game upgrade. Dense, dark fabric interwoven with spell-resistant thread.
  * Reduces incoming magical damage specifically
  * Essential once demonic sorcery becomes a regular threat inside the castle.
* The Dead Wizard's Robe — Found deep in the castle ruins or dungeons (somewhere "off the beaten path").
  * Clearly belonging to someone powerful and long gone.
  * Offers strong physical and magical protection.

## Head

* Apprentice's Cap — Your starting headwear.
* Circlet of Warding — A thin iron band inscribed with protective glyphs.
  * Minor boost to phsyical and magical defense.
* Cowl of the Unseen — A deep hood enchanted to make the player less likely to alert enemies.
  * Allows for a stealthier approach to some areas of the game.

## Hands / Arms

* Spellbinder Gloves — Fingerless leather gloves wrapped in copper wire.
  * Slightly increase spell casting speed and reduce mana cost on rapid-fire wands.
* Gauntlet of Deflection — A single heavy gauntlet worn on the off hand.
  * Greatly increases the odds of a physical attack being deflected.
  * No boost to defense.

## Feet

* Swiftstrider Boots — Enchanted leather boots that reduce movement noise and increase movement speed slightly.

# Items

## Accessories / Bound Items

* Talisman of the Ancestors — A carved bone pendant.
  * Passively regenerates a tiny amount of health over time — slow enough to be meaningless in active combat, meaningful between encounters.
* The Wizard's Own Amulet — Recovered from the drowned wizard's corpse.
  * A powerful protective amulet.
  * Its protection grows the longer it is held.

## Consumable Items

* Tincture of Mending — Your basic health potion.
  * A common alchemical remedy available from the town apothecary and found throughout levels.
* Draught of Vitality — A stronger, rarer healing item. Full or near-full restore.
  * Found in significant locations — boss rooms, hidden caches — never casually.
* Bloodmoss Compress — A slow heal over time rather than instant.
* Mana Vial -- restores mana; most common item in the game.
* Concentrated Essence — A dense, rare mana item that fully restores a single weapon's ammunition.
  * Found near weapons themselves, encouraging a discovery loop.
* Ghostlight Torch — Illuminates dark areas and reveals invisible or hidden enemies for a short duration.
  * Essential in the dungeon stretch where the architecture grows deliberately oppressive.
* Sorcerer's Salve — Temporarily amplifies spell damage for a short window.
* Leaf of the Nimble — A temporary speed boost, both movement and spell casting rate.
* Bread — given to the player by the couple they rescued in the tutorial.
  * Restores a small amount of health and mana simultaneously.
* Shard of the Relic — A fragment broken from the wizard's confiscated artifact during the transaction.
  * Might prove useful later...

# Partnership Agreement Template for Indie Game Studio Co-Founders

**Disclaimer:** This is a basic template for a partnership agreement between two co-founders of a small indie game studio. It is not legal advice and should not be used as a substitute for professional legal counsel. Please have this document reviewed and customized by a qualified lawyer before signing to ensure it meets your specific needs and complies with applicable laws.

## 1. Parties Involved
- Co-Founder 1: [Name]
- Co-Founder 2: [Name]
- Studio Name: [Studio Name]

## 2. Revenue Split
- All revenues from the studio's games and related activities will be split equally (50/50) between the co-founders.
- This includes profits from sales, licensing, merchandise, and any other income sources.
- Expenses will be deducted before splitting revenues.
- Adjustments to the split can be made by mutual agreement in writing.

## 3. Intellectual Property Ownership
- All intellectual property (IP) created for the studio, including games, code, art, music, and trademarks, will be jointly owned by both co-founders.
- Each co-founder retains ownership of any pre-existing IP they bring to the partnership.
- Any new IP created during the partnership will be assigned to the studio entity (if formed) or jointly owned.

## 4. Decision-Making Authority
- Major decisions, such as releasing a new game, entering partnerships, or hiring key staff, require unanimous agreement from both co-founders.
- Day-to-day operational decisions can be made by either co-founder, but major changes must be discussed and agreed upon.
- In case of a tie, decisions will be deferred to a neutral third party or mediator.

## 5. What Happens If One Partner Leaves
- If a co-founder voluntarily leaves, they will forfeit their share of future revenues but may retain a portion of past earnings based on their contribution.
- The remaining co-founder will have the option to buy out the departing partner's share at fair market value, as determined by an independent appraisal.
- If a co-founder is forced out due to breach of agreement, they may lose all rights to IP and revenues.
- The agreement will outline a process for valuation and transfer of assets.

## 6. Dispute Resolution
- Any disputes will first be resolved through direct negotiation between the co-founders.
- If negotiation fails, disputes will be submitted to mediation by a neutral third party.
- If mediation is unsuccessful, disputes will be resolved through binding arbitration in [Jurisdiction], avoiding court litigation.
- All parties agree to keep disputes confidential.

## 7. General Provisions
- This agreement is effective as of [Date] and continues until terminated by mutual agreement or as outlined above.
- Amendments must be in writing and signed by both parties.
- Governing law: [State/Country] laws.
- Signatures: [Space for signatures]
